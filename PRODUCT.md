# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary users are potential enterprise customers, executives, budget owners, AI project sponsors, and strategic partners evaluating whether Deng Xuede can help move an AI initiative from idea, demo, or POC into budget approval and scaled rollout. Secondary users include AI commercialization peers, sales leaders, recruiters, and collaborators looking for evidence of judgment, operating method, and enterprise deal experience.

Users arrive in a scanning mindset: they need to quickly understand credibility, commercial outcomes, point of view, and whether this person is worth contacting.

## Product Purpose

NeonMe is Deng Xuede's personal brand site for AI commercialization and enterprise AI sales. It exists to turn a personal profile into a memorable commercial intelligence brand: strong point of view, proof of large enterprise outcomes, case dossiers, operating principles, and field notes.

Success means visitors remember the positioning, understand the business value of Deng's AI sales experience, trust the deal and ROI claims, and know how to request a briefing or consultation.

## Brand Personality

Bold, youthful, commercially sharp, and intelligence-driven. The brand should feel like an AI commercial war room meets an editorial personal media property: energetic and memorable, but still credible for enterprise buyers.

Tone should be direct, opinionated, strategic, and outcome-focused. It should avoid generic inspiration language and speak in the language of ROI, POC, CFO buy-in, discovery, and scale.

## Anti-references

Do not look like a generic AI SaaS landing page with cyan grids, glass cards everywhere, gradient text, and interchangeable startup copy. Do not look like a traditional executive résumé site, conservative corporate brochure, gaming cyberpunk interface, or decorative portfolio with visuals disconnected from business evidence.

Avoid: generic dark-tech templates, excessive glassmorphism, decorative glitch effects, hero metric clichés, identical card grids, meaningless neon, emoji-as-icons, and vague claims without business context.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with a strong commercial thesis before credentials.
2. Every visual effect must carry business meaning: signal, dossier, proof, operating model, or contact path.
3. Make enterprise credibility feel energetic rather than conservative.
4. Treat cases as deal dossiers, not portfolio cards.
5. Keep the visitor path clear: understand the thesis, verify proof, read the method, request a briefing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The site prioritizes visual impact, but must remain usable: readable Chinese body text, clear CTA targets, mobile-safe layouts, keyboard focus states, and a reduced-motion fallback for scanning, ticker, and ambient motion. Minimum practical target is readable contrast for key content and no motion that blocks reading or navigation.
